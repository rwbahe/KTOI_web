Accounting & Finance

The Finance and Administration offices oversees various Tribal affairs, ranging from financial services and working with the Gaming Commission to economic development, housing, transportation, Indian Child Welfare, Tribal Employment Rights, Tribal Court and community liaison efforts. These offices work closely with the Tribal Council to promote economic development for Tribal enterprices.

The Administrative Director also serves as coordinator for the Kootenai Valley Resource Initiative, in partnership with the City of Bonners Ferry and Boundary County. This cooperative local effort seeks to restore the social, cultural, economic and natural resource bases in the community, as well as provide a forum to address natual reosurces, wildlife, wetland conservation and water quality issues.

Legal

The Legal Department provides legal advice and services to the Council, Departments and Enterprise to honor the Covenant by protecting Tribal sovereignty and Treaty right, promoting Tribal self-determination and self-governance. The Department, directed by the Attorney General, reports directly to the Tribal Council and works closely with other Departments and Enterprises to assist in achieving the Tribe's goals.

The Department coordinates with other governments in Ktunaxa Territory and surrounding areas, including other Ktunaxa governments, sister Indigenous Nations, Federal governments of the United States and Canada, and State and Provincal governments to promote Ktunaxa values and strengthen intergovernmnetal relations. The Legal Department acts as the Kootenai Tribe of Idaho Law and Order Code (Kootenai Code) Reviser.
